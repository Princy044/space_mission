1. Analysis of the problem

In past year, the number of spacecrafts launched has increased exponentially. All countries have grown more dependent on space technology for telecommunication, military and earth observation. In modern days, space mission are focusing on exciting sciences like a pursuit of finding water and life in other planets. However, the space environment is harsh and not very predictable. This calls for a meticulous and very challenging design for the spacecraft. The heart and brain of spacecraft or rover are electronics, batteries and computers which should be guarded for a long term space mission. Generally, for the design of electronic circuit for commercial applications takes into account component parameter variations due to initial tolerance, temperature, and aging. For space applications, the additional component parameter variation should be considered due to radiation. In outer space most of the semiconductor electronics are damaged by total ionizing dose (TID) caused by space- radiation and single event effect (SEE) caused by high energy particle radiation [6]. The charged particles in space radiation environment consist primarily of high-energy electrons, protons, alpha particles and heavy ions (cosmic rays) [1]. Harsh radiation can induce electrical charging in spacecraft materials. Large particle currents are carried by solar winds which when interacts with planetary magnetosphere creates high energy particle belt around certain planets like Earth, Jupiter and Saturn. When spacecraft passes through this belt can charge spacecraft material with internal fields which causes damaging electrostatic discharges [2]. The other extreme will be very low space temperature for deep space application which will affect the spacecraft electronics functionality and degradation in parametric performance. For example, a spacecraft launched to explore the rings of Saturn would experience temperature of -183 C [7] for other planets given in table. 1. These effects can cause reduced mission life to complete function failure [3]. In addition to ionizing radiation, tunnelling stress and hot carriers generates Joule heating in a gate electrode [4].

1.1. Main problem analysis

The high-energy particles in radiation environment can penetrate the spacecraft material and deposit kinetic energy. This process causes atomic displacement which leads to decreased power production by solar and failure in electronic components. In June 1993, European Space Agency (ESA) Hippocras astronomy satellite experienced trouble in communication due to component failure caused by radiation and was shut down [12].

Long term ionization effect is caused when proton or charged particle enters the spacecraft material and interact with an electron causes some atoms to become ionized to form electron-hole pair. This damage can occur in semiconductors as well as insulators the difference lies in whether this effect dissipates or accumulates. Total ionization dose (TID) is defined as total energy per unit mass of material, transferred to the material via ionization from all ionizing particles [2]. TID effects spacecraft devices and materials such as MOS devices, BJT transistors, analog microcircuits, digital microcircuits, quarts resonant crystal and external polymer surface. When a single energetic particle like high energy proton or heavy ion causes an event it is known as single event effect (SEE) [14]. Almost half of the failure in spacecraft are electrical in nature which is shown in Fig. 1.

2.1.1Silicon nanowire (SiNW) Gate-all-around (GAA) field effect transistor (FET):

This ultimate CMOS devices in terms of electrostatic, gate-all-around device has gate placed on all four sides of the channel. It is silicon nanowire with gate going all around it

[5].GAA has proven to have inherent radiation immunity compared to the SOI-based FET and FinFET [4], [5]. In 10ns the temperature of the gate can supress 900C which is sufficient to remove trapped charges and interface traps in the gate dielectric [4]. SiNW GAA FET is said to have a self-healing process which is showed in Fig. 2. When the device is effected by space problems like radiation, tunnelling stress and aging, the high energy particle generate fixed charges and are interface trapped in the dielectric. This aging process leads to failure in semiconductor based electronics but when voltage is applied to the gate electrode, the gate dielectric and isolation dielectric are annealed by temperature generated by Joule heating shown in Fig.2. This results in self-healing process. Thus the damaged device is recovered for a fresh start [4].

2.1.2 Gallium arsenide (GaAs) epitaxy lift-off (ELO) cells

GaAs is the second most used semiconductor material [1]. GaAs forms a face centred cube lattice with one gallium atom and one arsenide atom which is known as Zincblende. GaAs are used for their electrical and optical property. GaAs electron mobility comparatively higher than silicon [1]. The electron mobility of GaAs is a function of temperature, which changes depending on the intensity of device operation. In addition GaAs has good radiation resistance, moisture resistance, flexible, lightweight and low temperature coefficient [20][1]. Due to all this factors GaAs can be used in solar cells for space applications and they also perform good at low light [20]. Space compatible thin-film solar cells which are separated from GaAs substrate by epitaxial lift-off (ELO) as shown in fig.3. The solar cells are grown by low pressure metalorganic vapour phase epitaxy (MOVPE) at temperature 650C and pressure 20 mbar h. Trimethylgallium, trimethylindium and trimethylaluminium are used as group-III precursors and Arsine and phosphine were used as group-V [21]. GaAs ELO cells are cemented between two glass plates as shown in fig.3. The cover glass undergoes dehydration at 180C for half an hour before cementing them together, after experimenting this process has proved to be successful because there was no micro-cracking or delamination after irradiation [21]. In paper [21] three ELO solar cells where mounted on aluminium carbon honeycomb structure and 600 thermal cycling test where performed with temperature range of -80 to +160C. None of the cells showed any visible damage but no current was detected in one cell, this might be because of loss in contact between metal tab and epoxy. However, this GaAs ELO cells show lesser disadvantage compared to that of the traditional GaAs on a Ge substrate.

2.1.3 Devices for extreme low temperature:

The operation of electronics at low temperature depends on the type of electronic components used. Some devices like bipolar junction transistors (BJT) are subject to carrier freeze with low temperature since the excitation rely on thermal conduction. On the other hand, Metal Oxide Semiconductor Field effect transistor (MOSFET) can operate at cryogenic temperature [10]. The performance of MOSFET increases with decrease in the temperature as shown in Fig. 4 due to increase in carrier mobility, higher threshold voltage, steeper sub threshold slope, higher trans conductance, reduced junction leakage and supressed complementary CMOS latch up [10][11]. The increase in trans conductance as a function of temperature is due to increase in mobility with decreasing temperature. The threshold voltage increases positively for n-channel and negatively for p- channel but the overall gain of MOSFET increases [11].

2.1.4 Lightweight electronics packaging

The lifetime of gallium arsenide and silicon semiconductor devices decreases threefold with every 10K increase in temperature [17]. To have good thermal management, cold plates or heat sinks made of high thermal conductivity are used in packaging basic structure is show in fig.5. Effective heat sinking is required to enable close packing of the circuitry to make it smaller. If the thermal expansion of packaging material is very different from that of substrate material it causes mismatch stress which leads to thermo-mechanical fatigue consequently premature aging. The ideal material for space application packaging should have Coefficient of Thermal Expansion (CTE) same or slightly higher than that of the substrate material, high Thermal Conductivity (TC) and low density [19]. Advanced materials are formed combining low CTE with high TC like CuW or CuMo has high TC and tailored CTE. Two such alloys are Kovar and Inver has low TC and high density which make them not applicable for space applications. The outer hermetic seal are made of ceramics and metals. If the right materials and polymers are used for packaging it can maintain a well-controlled environment for electronics up to 20 years of product life [18].

2.1.5 Thermal control methods for spacecraft

Improving design of electronics is not enough to remove hot spots completely. Therefore an effective heat removal is also required to ensure the efficiency of semi-conductor electronics operating at such extreme temperatures.

10

High Temperature Electronics

2.1.5.1 Multilayer insulation (MLI)

MLI blankets protects the spacecraft from external excessive heat and also minimise the heat loss from the inside. The blankets consists of multilayers of polished radiation shielding. Made of thin Mylar sheets which are aluminized on both sides to behave as low- conductivity spacer. For higher performance the MLIs are made of metalized Mylar films with gold or aluminium and covered on both side with Dacron or silk for low-conductance spacer [22]. MLI acts as radiation shielding which are highly reflective and low emittance. The charged particles in the space can charge the spacecraft to a potential of 11000 V. The high dielectric constant and high capacitance nature of the aluminized Mylar blankets allows to store such potential. Through emitting sparks in the surrounding the blankets discharge there potential [22].

2.1.5.2. Heat pipes

Heat pipes are sealed tubes made of high thermal conductivity materials like aluminium or copper. It transfers large amount of heat between hot and cold interfaces. Inside the vacuum tube is a working fluid filled in fraction depending on the operating temperature. Inside the tube at hot interface the liquid is converter to gas which normally flows and condenses at cold interface as shown in fig. 6. Working fluid should be chosen considering the material used for the pipes, in case of chemical reaction it could be dangerous. The operating temperature determines the fluid used. Some of the working fluids which can be used are mercury, ethanol, sodium, acetone or water [22], [24].

2.1.5.3. Radiators

Radiators are used to reject excess heat produced in the spacecraft by different components or by environment, in form of Infrared Radiation (IR) through there surfaces. The amount of radiating power depends on temperature and surface emittance. Radiators surface are given low solar absorptance and high IR emittance for maximum heat rejection [22] [23].

2.1.5.4 Heaters

Heaters are used deep space missions where MLI are not sufficient to maintain temperature. The heaters are usually combined with thermostat or solid state controllers to maintain precise temperature. Foil heaters are flexible heating elements made of Nichrome or Econol used on flat surfaces. Foil heaters are fixed on electronic packaging, spacecraft panels using pressure sensitive adhesives. Tape heaters enamelled heating elements sandwiched between glass clothes used on curved surfaces [22] [23].

3. Conclusion

In this paper we discuss material, device, packaging and thermal control solutions.

If the electronic device and material solutions function independently at extreme temperature without extra thermal control requirement the weight and cost of the spacecraft will be reduced drastically. However, long term functionality of the device at extreme temperature are not assured without thermal control in spacecraft which is difficult for deep space missions. We discussed about material solutions which function independently and also thermal control solutions. For future human space travel we need highly efficient electronics as well as thermal control will increase the cost drastically.
